## CHAPTER BREAKDOWN: Protagonist / Motivation / Obstacle / Antagonist

### FRAME PROLOGUE: Father, I Fear (~2,500 words)
- **Voice:** Free verse, liturgical, confessional
- **Protagonist:** The Son (Ajita, before he knows his name)
- **Motivation:** Overcome fear to act
- **Obstacle:** Paralyzing dread — of death, time, failure, loneliness
- **Antagonist:** Fear itself (Pà is not antagonist — he is mirror)
- **Ends with:** "A story of Love in the time of the Pain Lord, on an old planet called Hassan."

## VOICE RULES (from VOICE-GUIDE.md + Nora's notes)

### Per-Section Voice:
- **Island (Ch 1-4):** Close third. Sensory precision + philosophical reach. Understatement.
- **City (Ch 5-8):** Maximalist. Accelerating. Blunt dialogue. Kinetic.
- **Earth 2069 (Ch 9-10, 13-14):** Multiple sub-registers. Mars Mother = tender first person. A Consultation = corporate-poetic. The Heist = noir-kinetic.
- **The Turn (Ch 11-12):** Island + City dissolving. Interior. The registers bleed.
- **Hassan (Ch 15-26, 29-33):** Lush. Physical. Formal dialogue. Natural metaphor. Combat = dance.
- **Hyfe (Ch 27-28):** Cold. Precise. Monastic predator. Calm intensity.
- **Frame (Prologue, Epilogue):** Free verse. Liturgical. Naked. No ironic distance.

### Nora's Top 5 (hold throughout):
1. **Watch your tense** — pick one, hold it
2. **Cut descriptors** — one detail, not four (Diane von Furstenburg)
3. **Embody, don't narrate** — the body in the world, not the mind describing it
4. **Signal transitions** — don't make the reader reorient without a breadcrumb
5. **One comparison per beat** — classical references and figurative language need room to breathe

### Style Rules (from Principles of Style):
- Omniscient third POV: camera zooms in/out of character heads
- Baseline narrator: Mythic melancholy, lucid brutality, quiet yearning, fatal grace, faithful triumph
- Action sequences: 2 paragraphs max. 4-6 for outstanding fights.
- Em-dashes: create pause, emphasis, contrast, interruption. Don't overuse.
- Never over-explain. Trust the reader.
- Keep it lean. Every beat drives the story.
