# HASSAN — Structural Architecture Plan

## Context

The novel Hassan exists as a rich but fragmented collection of scenes, chapters, and world-building across a Scrivener project. The author's vision: a nested, multi-layered epic where the **Planet Hassan** (far-future epic fantasy) is the outermost story, and **The City/Island** (the Ainos Enigram timeline) is the inner story — a past that explains how Hassan's world came to be. The near-future **Earth** material is the deepest past, revealed through memory and flashback.

The goal: structure all existing material into a continuous, scene-by-scene plan with a rubric for each scene, treating the novel like a large codebase — modular, testable, architecturally sound.

---

## Structural Principle: The Braid

**Not** linear. **Not** frame-within-frame. A **braid** — two timelines running in parallel, alternating chapters, each ending on a hook that pulls you into the other timeline. Like Crichton's multi-POV escalation, but with two entire worlds.

**Timeline A (OUTER): HASSAN** — Far-future epic fantasy. Aharah, the Haz King, the Oracle, the Tagata quest, the Clone Wars. This is the "present tense" of the novel.

**Timeline B (INNER): THE AINOS ENIGRAM** — The Island, The City, The Oukemene. Valentine, Vera, Pace, the Maestro. This is the deep past that *created* Hassan's world.

**Timeline C (DEEPEST): EARTH** — Marcos, Nolen Dawn, the Box, the telomere plague. Revealed through flashbacks, dreams, and the Professor's appearances *within* Timeline B. Never gets its own chapters — it bleeds through.

**The connection:** The Lyonessa in Hassan IS the mythic echo of Vera/the V-line. The Ainos Enigram IS Mater. The Tagata IS the unfinished work. The reader discovers this gradually.

## Scene Rubric (applied to every scene)

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **#** | Scene number (e.g., A1, B1, A2, B2...) |
| **Title** | Working title |
| **Timeline** | A (Hassan), B (Enigram), C (Earth flashback within B) |
| **Setting** | Where and when, in 1-2 lines |
| **POV Character** | Whose eyes we see through |
| **Characters Present** | Who else is in the scene |
| **Motivation** | What does the POV character want RIGHT NOW? |
| **Plotline** | What happens — the action, in 2-4 lines |
| **Theme** | What idea does this scene explore? |
| **Hook** | How does it end to make you turn the page? |
| **Audience Interest** | Why does a reader care? (tension, mystery, emotion, spectacle) |
| **Writerly Interest** | Is the prose alive? What's the stylistic ambition? |
| **Story Interest** | Is this exciting? Crichton test: would you read this on a plane? |
| **Status** | DRAFTED / OUTLINED / BLANK — does prose exist in Scrivener? |
| **Source** | Scrivener document title(s) this pulls from |

Each scene file contains:

2. The prose — either pulled from existing Scrivener text (converted to markdown) or marked NEEDS DRAFTING
3. Editorial notes (what works, what needs revision, connections to other scenes)

## Verification / Quality Checks

1. **Continuity check**: Every character introduced must have a prior establishment or a deliberate mystery
2. **Hook check**: Every scene must end on a question, threat, or emotional charge that pulls into the next
3. **Braid check**: No more than 2 consecutive scenes in the same timeline (A-B-A-B rhythm, with exceptions for Part 4's City sequence)
4. **Crichton test**: Read each scene cold — would you keep reading on a plane?
5. **Thematic resonance**: Each A-scene should echo or contrast its paired B-scene
6. **Earth bleed-through**: Timeline C should never get its own scene — it surfaces through dreams, the Professor, flashbacks
