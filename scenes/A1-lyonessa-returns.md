## Prose

Anais of the Jian clan waited for her sister, Aharah's, return along the waterfront. The waves roiled jaggedly. Herons and gulls glided along the water's surface placid as can be while hairs of rain turned into a moonsoon's downpour before letting up again gently. Large, thick blankets of grey clouds covered the sky and when the winds blew just right, the clouds opened a portal to let in light and it looked like some angelic eye.

"Auntie" waited with Anais, dressed in her purple green upturned skirt with corrugated pleats near her waist. Her blouse was equally eccentric and asymetrical. The ancient automaton had been with the family since Anais could remember and her great-grandparents had told her "auntie" could be hundreds if not thousands of years old— she could even be one of the original. Her bronzen face had turned green with time and her cheeks glistened from the many small Jian hands that burnished them. Anais wore a simple, eggshell-colored unitard that warded off the rain and kept her warm. She released the transparent hood that covered her face and despite auntie's protest, she relished in the freshness and the coolness of falling water. The bits of rain stung, and Anais smiled.

The sky winked and darkened. A battered figure turned around the bend of the road from Eastward. She was covered in dirty rags and limped on a leg. Both hands were wrapped in muddied cloth that had been shredded from her midrift. Anais could see her ribs protruding from her thin skin that shone golden in places even in the twilight of the morning.

"The Lyonessa," auntie said with urgency in her voice. But she stayed standing where she was, eventhough her body propelled forward as if magnetized toward her once vibrant and hard-headed ward.

"Go to her," Anais said, her voice quiet and soft, tinged with longing. Released from standing, Auntie was off, sprinting faster than a leopard. Her thick pleats cut through the air like fins.

## Editorial Notes

- **What works:** The opening image is cinematic — the waterfront, the rain, the "angelic eye" in the clouds. Auntie is immediately compelling (ancient automaton in eccentric clothes, bronzed face turned green with age). The reunion is visceral. Aharah's golden sunskin visible through rags is a striking visual.
- **What needs work:** "moonsoon" → "monsoon." Some sentences run long ("The ancient automaton had been with the family since Anais could remember and her great-grandparents had told her..."). Tighten. The Mara reveal needs to be SPECTACULAR — this is the reader's first view of a Hassan city. Linger on it.
- **Connections:** Auntie connects to the automaton theme in Timeline B (she may be one of the "originals" — a deep-past V-line?). The golden sunskin = photosynthetic skin, a Shakyan biological technology. The Mara illusion = "Maya" (Buddhist illusion) as both city name and defensive technology.
- **Pair with:** B1 (The Replacematon). Both scenes are about strangers arriving — Aharah returning broken, Valentine arriving fresh and arrogant. Mirror structure.
