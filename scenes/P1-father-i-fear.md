### Key Lines (confirmed from source):

- "I fear, if I give it my all, I'll die, / but holding in reserve, I'm unsatisfied."
- "Am nothing — / I fear I have taken the wrong path."
- "You're never alone. / Forces great and small, / visible and invisible, / hold you and carry you. / But you must ask. / Merely ask."
- "It is the story of Love in the time of the Pain Lord, on an old planet called Hassan."

## Editorial Notes

- **What works:** The confessional intimacy. The litany of fears is universal. The payoff — "merely ask" — is earned. The final line is a perfect door into a vast world.
- **What needs work:** Consider whether the "Daughter of Dawn" reference lands without context. It might — it's mysterious enough to intrigue.
- **Connections:** "The Pain Lord" = the Haz King / Dhukkha Khan. "The Daughter of Dawn" = likely Lola Dawn or a figure in the Dawn dynasty. The frame returns in E1 (Epilogue).
- **Length target:** 1-2 pages. This should be SHORT. A punch, not a speech.
