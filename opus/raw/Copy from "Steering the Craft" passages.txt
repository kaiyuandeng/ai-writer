Hackers find an exploit whilst building a money transfer application. They were bouncing around one single dollar to test the integrity of their fund transfer when the dollar disappeared mid-transfer.
“This doesn’t happen,” one said.
“This can’t happen, check again. The banks don’t make this sort of mistakes.”
“They’ll think we disappeared the dollar when it doesn’t add up to one.”
“Let them worry ‘bout that, quick, start another transfer.”
“Another transfer? When the first…”
“So we can test the infra. You want to fail at two jobs?”
“Right, right, the infra. Another Pong is on the way.”
“Ping. Good, got it. And if this one goes disappears…”
The other grinned. “Already on that haus—I added a tracer to the logging algo so when it—”
The single dollar disappeared again, as if on cue.
“There! It blinked! What does the log say?”
“There, the trace ends at Vallejo Sector.”
They rock, paper, and scissored. One puts out rock. It is beaten by the Other’s paper. The Other grins and hands the One a space-helm.
“Make sure to seal that one tight,” the Other said, “the seal can leak around the glass inset.”
The One gave him a dirty look. “Shut up and give me that helmet. So wordy all of a sudden.”
The Other handed One the helmet, which One studiously donned—quickly and with minimal effort. The helmet formed a helmetic seal with a sound like a swoosh that ended too quick. One was sealed. The Other gave him a thumbs-up; One replied; Their conversation link momentarily scrambled by the entrance into the Old Matrix.
