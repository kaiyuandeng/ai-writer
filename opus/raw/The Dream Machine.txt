The Dream Machine
A short story in the Hassan cosmos


	They found it on Mars, brewed from Cosmic Milk. They found it in Space, in the Ether, in a vacuum where nothing is. They found it, running experiments in collosal buried compute “cities” that would make New York blush. They wrung it from the webbings of atoms, from splitting, fusing, squeezing, compressing, and unleashing. A careful hand without a human heart guided their endeavors and in the later part of the decade 2050, they—Martians—opened a spigot, tapped into the tangible and invisible fabric of the Universe, mixed with a synthetic version of DMT extracted from the brain of a comatose girl who talked to them in their dreams, let flow a new chemistry and its starchild was called the Hala Hala, named for the deadliest poison extracted from the churning of the Ocean of Milk by the devas and asaras that threatened to choke the world. And in the legend, Lord God Vishnu sacrificed himself by drinking the poison; his consort constrained the poison to his threat; and the god became blue.
