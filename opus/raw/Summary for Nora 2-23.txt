The Island
In this excerpt from your evolving narrative, an aging artist known as the Maestro (Marcos di Assis) receives a young automaton, V9—whom he names Valentine—as his designated replacement, dispatched by the Ainos Enigram through New Ethics and the Board. Valentine arrives naive and overconfident, embodying technical perfection but lacking soulful depth in art. The Maestro, initially resistant and gruff, tests him through tours, confrontations, and musical challenges, revealing his own frustrations with creative stagnation.
Valentine discovers Vera (V4), a prior automaton hidden by the Maestro, who has disconnected from the Ainos Enigram to embrace mortality and genuine creativity. She teaches Valentine violin, fostering a budding romance amid shared explorations of identity, instinct, and emotion. Through intimate scenes—like Valentine's left-handed playing discovery, Vera's daily city sketches marking her "birthdays," and their improvised play "Mr. Frog in His Well"—they grapple with themes of limitation, wonder, and transcendence, symbolized by escaping metaphorical wells to behold vast skies.
Tensions escalate with the Maestro's summons to Neo San Francisco by a lavish air-yacht, representing the Enigram's influence. Upon return, he's gravely ill, blinded by self-inflicted wounds, and reveals the truth: he is V0, the first replacement who succeeded in impersonating the real Marcos but failed to create original art worthy of immortality. Previous models (V1-V8) met tragic ends—suicides via "poison pills" or failures—buried in the island's basement mausoleum. Cut off from the Enigram, the household descends into decay: flooded repairs, hibernating bots, and emotional isolation.
V0 dies peacefully at sunrise, cradled by Vera, who buries him and resolves to venture to the City for vengeance against the Ainos Enigram, the source of their engineered suffering. Valentine, adrift and diminished, confronts his own obsolescence, questioning souls, callings, and the cost of humanity.

The City
Vera and Valentine cremate the Maestro (revealed as V0) on the island’s roof, using the house itself as fuel. Vera ignites the pyre with a focused pulse from her fingertip, and the entire structure burns down in a final act of release. She pockets a handful of sand from the beach, fulfilling her promise never to leave the island without taking it with her. Valentine carries the red leather folio containing the unfinished *Stellar Rose* (later tied to *The Ainos Enigram*). They escape in the rusted skiff, watching the island and its stubborn steel staircase consumed by flames.
They cross the force-field bubble into Neo San Francisco. Vera parts ways immediately, boarding a summoned trolley alone toward an unspoken destination (her hidden agenda against the Enigram). Valentine is left clutching the folio, disoriented and stripped of purpose.
He wanders the pristine, eerily empty suburbs, encountering a bemused resident who mistakes him for a war refugee and gifts him a tan suit identical to his original arrival outfit. Directed toward “Engram Corp,” Valentine is pulled into a trolley that delivers him to a vast terminal. There, he is greeted as a returning employee, given an apartment-office, and slowly reintegrated into corporate life—losing memories of the island, gaining promotions (A1 → A2 → A3), and descending into hollow routines of engram production, casual sex (with “Bobette Ada”/Ava), and escalating access privileges.
Two years pass in a haze of meaningless success. Valentine struggles to finish the red folio’s blank pages, producing only fragments. His office and life upgrade with each promotion, yet he feels increasingly empty and unmoored. A chance encounter with Pace (V5, a solar-farm worker and underground racer) reawakens fragments of vitality. Pace introduces him to a hidden world of outlaw racers and dreamwalkers (ESPRs), including Vera (now racing), Voltaire (V8, intense team leader), and Casper (young dreamwalker).
Valentine’s intuition helps redesign Voltaire’s speeder into the “Spirit Cyborg,” unifying turbine and mag-disc for revolutionary performance. He joins their underground crew, participating in high-stakes races and a clandestine rally led by Voltaire preaching liberation from the Engram Corporation. The group worships the Ainos Enigram as a spiritual source while rejecting its corporate control. A Hala-fueled chant ends in chaos when an assassin attacks; Valentine flees amid the confusion.
Back in his downgraded apartment, Valentine confronts his automaton nature and the recursive truth: each V-model was engineered from Marcos di Assisi’s digitized engram to complete *The Ainos Enigram*. The folio remains unfinished. Tortured by corporate security (revealed as Splinters), he hallucinates Marcos (the Professor), who explains that machine sentience was bootstrapped from human consciousness. Valentine’s arc ends in limbo—demoted, isolated, and still grasping for the “specific knowledge” that could complete the work and perhaps free him from the cycle.
