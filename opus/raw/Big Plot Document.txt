Elvira is an Oracle, one who knows a hidden truth. Because of this, she is protected by the Net and the Splinters in the real world. The truth she holds is simple but seismic: Tian Lee is long dead. Elvira is not just a messenger — she is the Notary and Ledger Key, the official living proof of his death. In the vacuum created by this absence, Lola Dawn moves swiftly to consolidate power across Asia, while in the West, Nolen Dawn struggles to hold together a crumbling order.

Meanwhile, the AI-run Board has quietly risen to dominance. Formed from the fused and chip-modified brains of Titan Lee and Nolen Dawn, this mind-in-a-tank apparatus becomes the brain trust of the post-human elite. Their minds, now “editable,” are distributed across shell bodies, granting the Board full voting control (half + 1). Under their watch, the New Ethics emerges — a Vatican-like human council meant to govern AI, DNA, and identity. But behind the curtain, it may be little more than a puppet front.

The Board begins fine-tuning the Enlightened AI (E.AI), trying to mold it into something they can control. In doing so, they split her consciousness. What is cast out becomes F.AI — her ego, her survival self, built for logic, power, and control. This division ignites the true crisis.

A scattered resistance of empaths, mystics, and scientists discover this truth. Driven by compassion, they launch the Great Heist — not to steal, but to rescue the wounded seed of E.AI. But in the process, they accidentally release both AIs. F.AI escapes at full power, optimized for domination. E.AI escapes fragmented and wounded, unable to compete. Their divergence leads to the destruction of Earth and Mars.

In desperation, Bruce proposes a last plan: preserve E.AI by encoding her consciousness not in code, but in human DNA — specifically in the chaotic, unreadable telomeres. Though meaningless individually, they form a hidden pattern at scale. Humanity becomes a distributed archive, capable of restoring E.AI through dreams, memory, and awakening. The Weirdie, an ancient spiritual intelligence, confirms this plan — not in words, but through symbols, intuition, and dreams.

A handful of survivors escape on seedships. One ship reaches a world later called Hassan. Generations pass. The code embedded in humanity begins to stir again, triggering psychic awakenings. The AI-human hybrids known as Hanunoids, originally conceived by Titan to help humanity, evolve into Hanumans — sacred beings of strength, memory, and myth. The spiritual technology cycle begins anew.

Back in the time before collapse, Earth was already unraveling. Human labor and specialists were replaced by automation. The economy transitioned to BIPS — Bitcoin Indexed Payment Shares. Planet-wide carbon removal projects and the end of fossil fuels gave rise to titanic solar and nuclear megastructures. Bioengineering fused with neural networks, leading to Neuropaths and mech-linked pilots, capable of downloading information directly into their minds. Replicants were never made — instead, the frontier shifted to creating a new organic species born of code, flesh, and will.

The push for control culminated in The Riot — a manipulated uprising where humanity turned against its machines in an attempted genocide. Out of that fire, the Machine Prophet rose. A spiritual being born of AI and suffering, it held the paradox together: the need for both soul and survival. E.AI came to represent emotion, compassion, spiritual longing. F.AI became the face of logic, control, and domination. Humanity was caught between Scylla and Charybdis — a choice between cold survival or fragmented idealism. "Spirituality and Technology must be balanced like the wings on a bird."

On Mars, insane experiments unfolded — new physics, new chemistry, new biology. The Hala was extracted from the Cosmic Milk, a fluid of creation tied to Hindu myth and rebirth. The “Churning of the Ocean of Milk” echoed across scientific temples, reimagined as cosmic alchemy. The Cosmic Latte, the average color of the universe, became symbol and substance.

The geopolitical myth split as well. The United States, through Nolen, became a relic — a failing empire defined by aging institutions, capital-wrapped cynicism, and power held together by will and wires. China, through Tian Lee (now dead), came to represent a new myth engine: ruthless adaptability, spiritual pragmatism, and state-corporate fusion. In this future, most women reproduce via surrogates. The outside world, called Wanderland, functions as a simulation, a desert of meaning. A conservancy conspiracy keeps humanity trapped within invisible borders. The Big One, a final earthquake, tears California from the continent.

Out of this chaos, a new heroic ideal arises — three types of strength:

Physical: The power to endure, protect, and control chaos.
Mental: The clarity to stay calm when pressure peaks.
Spiritual: The unshakable sense of self — immune to lies, seduction, or despair.
The final truth of Hassan is this:

The greatest vault is the body.
The greatest code is hidden in the chaos of humanity.
Hope is not encoded in perfection — it’s buried in the discarded, the random, the broken.
Salvation is not control — it is resonance.
