“What do you know about the Vermilians, son?” The Haz King said, his voice deep, whole, and booming.
“That you defeated them, Father” Prince Lyon said without missing a beat knowing full well the answer his Father wanted to hear.
“Pah.”
The Haz King spat on the ground.
“I wiped them off the face of this earth and no Vermillian is alive today because of me. And do you know how I destroyed them?”
“By your strength, Father,” Lyon said, but now unsure of this new territory of questioning.
The Haz King laughed and his laugh rumbled the vast auditorium. The stone pillars and dome overhead amplified the sound. He gripped his fist, and the hand came together into a thing made of wrought iron and crushing power. He softened and turned his relaxed hand around like an artist studying his nails.
“Did you know, son, that the Vermillians were stronger? Stronger; Faster; Equally intelligent. And they needed no food except for an orange that grew in abundance, water, and the Sun to sustain them?”
Lyon gasped. He acted it out a bit out. Better to be reactive and overly pleasing than to give anything else away. “But Father, I thought they were peasants. The scrolls…”
“The scrolls,” the Haz King said.
Lyon thought. “Then Father, how did you vanquish such a powerful enemy?”
The Haz King looked down from his throne and his small son raised his head from his bow. “How would you?” The Haz King asked.
Lyon breathed in slowly. His opportunity had finally come. He had waited, studied, bidded his time for years, besting all the others. Now the Haz King was asking him a question: a moment he had imagined would come. He snickered out, careful to hide a smile. “Destroy the Vermillians Father? Well, I would destroy what sustains them.”
The Haz King leaned back into his throne. He tilted his head. Prince Lyon looked up; The Haz King’s jaw seemed cut from stone; His neck was the size of a bouldre. And the Haz King’s eyes, concentrated, relaxed, and small, stared back.
“Poison the water. Blight the berries. Block out the Sun.” Lyon said.
The Haz King flared his nostrils and narrowed his eyes in approval. Joy surged through Lyon and he was good to hide it. He stared blankly at the Haz King’s feet, gently bowing his back and tilting his head. He received his approval silently.
“And that’s what we did,” the Haz King said. “Except…”
“Except what, Father?”
“Except not every Vermillian was killed. There remains one family, a father and two daughters.”
“Yes, Father.”
“I want you to find them.”
“And what shall I do when I find them?”
“Do what I would do.”
“Yes, Father. And what would be my reward?”
The Haz King scoffed. “You dare ask for reward?”
The Prince Lyon nodded his head with respect. “That’s what you would do, Father.”
The Haz King laughed. The room boomed. “You would have the Kingdom, son. How would you like to have your own kingdom? Going and spawn. Not longer little Prince Lyon. How would you like to be the “Lion King: Lyon” Son?”
Prince Lyon nodded, savoring every gesture, every breath, every vibration with a smile. Crossing his hand over his chest, taking a vow, he said: “I would like that very much, Father. I shall do that, in the name of the Haz King. Namaha.”
The Haz King brushed his hand and Lyon rose and made his way to the exit.

“I don’t know,” he said. He braced himself.
The Haz King nodded. “Good. Humility. It keeps you honest; honesty keeps you strong.”
A spark of inspiration lit up in Lyon’s mind. The answer was plainly buried in his father’s reply.

The Haz King nodded.
