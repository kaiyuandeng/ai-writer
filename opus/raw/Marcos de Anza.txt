Marcos has the walk of a martial artist—spring-loaded, private, and confident. His eyes are usually down-cast or casted aside. Each step is parallel in front of the other, and when he rests, he rests on one leg, with the other one bent picturesque at the knee with the foot lounging on the floor. This is the man who wrote the Stellar Rose. Composed the symphony. Directed the opera. And finally sold the rights to Magic Incoporated and has since then been living on a private, man-made island off the coast of San Francisco called the Dragon Den. Yes—his friends tried to talk him out of it, but he made the case the the Fortress of Solitude would not be any better. They debated. They say he’s comparing himself to superman. He’s saying superman has superperson priviledges. They say Dragon has too many racial connotations. Solitude is more vague. Marcos has a rare moment of personal collection and say they are right. So then when the news reported of any parties or burglaries, it was as “Marco’s De Anza’s debaucherous balchelor pad where he may have spliced and cloned a dragon. And not a dinosaur. A dragon, a serpent that breathes fire…” which officially and unofficially had a much cooler tone to it. But privately, they called it the Dragon Den. Or just the Island.
