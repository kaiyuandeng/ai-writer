# Movement IX: The Way Home

**Question:** What does it cost to see everything?

## The Ending

Anais goes blind. Not from injury — from seeing too much. Her astromancy, her connection to the Wyrd, her psychic gift — it burns out her physical eyes and opens omniscience. She can see in space. She can see the way Home.

The buried seedship — the Pacemaker — has been running Hassan's magnetosphere since the first crash landing. It is found in the planet's core. It is the ship.

But extracting it kills the planet. The core stops spinning. The magnetosphere collapses. The twin suns' solar wind scours Hassan bare.

Anais navigates. The coordinates are embedded in the myth:
**3 - 25 - 15 - 21 - 23 - 1**
These encode the word for Peace/Balance/Harmony — the author's mother's name.

They go Home.

## Key Constraints
- This should be 15,000-20,000 words maximum. Controlled. Devastating. Not epic — intimate.
- Anais's voice must carry: she has been the precocious younger sister for 200,000 words. Now she is the navigator. The shift must feel earned.
- The planet's death should echo the Island's burning in Movement I. Vera burned one house; Anais lets a world burn. Same gesture, cosmic scale.
- The coordinates reveal should feel like a lock clicking open — not a puzzle solved, but a recognition. Oh. It was always there.
