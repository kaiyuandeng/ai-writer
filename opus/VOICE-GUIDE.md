# VOICE GUIDE — The Registers of Hassan

The author writes in at least five distinct voices, each bound to a temporal plane and emotional register. Before drafting or editing any scene, identify which voice governs.

---

## 1. ISLAND VOICE (Movements I, III)
**Register:** Close third person that gradually merges with Valentine's interiority
**Signature moves:**
- Sensory precision married to philosophical reach ("He stroked the curve of the neck with the groove of his hand")
- Compressed metaphor, often from food or music ("dead cheese wrapped in cellophane" / "bacteria in it")
- Body-as-instrument: Valentine's degrading powers tracked through temperature readings, healing rates, sensor data
- Theatrical rhythm: scenes structured like acts with clear entrances, exits, beats
- The author's emotional restraint — devastating moments delivered through understatement ("It was very nice for a first time")
- Bracketed compositional notes appear in draft — these are artifacts of a writer in conversation with himself

**Key frequencies:** intimacy, shame, wonder, grief, humor-through-gentleness
**What to avoid:** exposition dumps, dialogue that explains what the reader already sees

---

## 2. CITY VOICE (Movement II)
**Register:** Maximalist, sensory, philosophically digressive — Bolaño meets cyberpunk
**Signature moves:**
- The prose accelerates with Valentine's hedonism and slows during his paralysis
- Blunt, colloquial dialogue grounds the cosmic themes ("Hot damn" / "What are you doing, you can't smoke here")
- Kinetic action sequences (the night drive, the underground racing) written in long breathless sentences that simulate velocity
- Corporate-surreal: the City's eerily cheerful infrastructure described as both seductive and suffocating
- Tonal shifts: comedy → dread → euphoria → despair in the span of a page
- The Professor's appearances break the register — his voice is British-academic, impish, Socratic

**Key frequencies:** velocity, dislocation, hunger, spectacle, loneliness-in-crowds
**What to avoid:** letting the philosophical digressions stop the story (they should float on the current, not dam it)

---

## 3. HASSAN VOICE (Movements IV, V, VI)
**Register:** Lush medieval-fantasy with internal monologue shifts, epic and physical
**Signature moves:**
- Sensory landscape writing: wind, sand, light, the smell of beetle paste, the sound of twin suns
- Dialogue has a formal quality — not archaic, but elevated, as if every conversation is slightly ceremonial
- Aharah's interior monologue is stubborn, concrete, practical — she thinks in problems and solutions, not abstractions
- The Haz King speaks in riddles and confessions, like a man talking to God and getting no answer
- Combat is choreographed like dance: directional, rhythmic, with emotional beats replacing blow-by-blow
- Natural metaphor: the desert, elephants, beetles, hydras, the twin suns — the landscape IS the psychology

**Key frequencies:** endurance, duty, shame, sisterly love, martial beauty, the sacred in the physical
**What to avoid:** generic fantasy cadence (no "forsooth" or "ere long"), no Tolkien pastiche

---

## 4. EARTH VOICE (Movement VII)
**Register:** Multiple sub-registers, all grounded in a recognizable near-future
**Sub-voices:**
- **Noir-detective:** First person, wisecracking, Chandler-indebted. "The bullshit detector started going off."
- **Domestic-tender:** Mars, Mother — first person, a child's directness, an adult's retrospective sorrow. Short paragraphs. Precise images. Emotional restraint that makes the gut-punches land harder.
- **Corporate-cyberpunk:** A Consultation, The Vulture — dense, poetic, the language of power and money. Tarot poems embedded in prose.
- **Comedy-of-errors:** A Shit Day — slapstick giving way to genuine tenderness. The walrus documentary. Henry's hamburger.
- **Hedonist-confessional:** The Binge in Kids — breathless, name-dropping, the prose of people drowning in pleasure

**Key frequencies:** nostalgia, menace, humor, grief-at-a-distance, the vertigo of wealth
**What to avoid:** letting any single Earth sub-voice dominate. The plane's power comes from its multiplicity.

---

## 5. FRAME VOICE (Prologue, Epilogue)
**Register:** Free verse, liturgical, confessional
**Signature moves:**
- Direct address: the Son speaks to Pà as a child speaks to God — intimate, desperate, brave
- Lists of fears that are deeply personal and contemporary ("I fear if I give it my all, I'll die")
- The volta: the shift from confession to command ("Then go! Son of Man…")
- The proper nouns arrive like detonations: "Hassan," "the Pain Lord," "the Daughter of Dawn"

**Key frequencies:** fear, prayer, courage, the sacred
**What to avoid:** self-consciousness, ironic distance. This voice is naked.

---

## Cross-Cutting Principles

1. **Compression is power.** The best lines in the manuscript are the shortest. "My blood is red." "It was very nice for a first time." "Mars, mother." Trust the reader.

2. **The body is the text.** Every character's interior state is legible through their body. Valentine's degrading senses. Aharah's bloodied fists on the rock. Pace's leg bent against the Firebird's tire. The Haz King's skin changing from obsidian to ivory. Anais's burning eyes. Never tell us how someone feels — show us where it lives in their flesh.

3. **Humor is not comic relief.** It's structural. The Maestro's insults, Pace's one-liners, the walrus documentary — they aren't breaks from the seriousness. They ARE the seriousness, wearing a different mask. "If machines could cry, their eyes would be full of rust."

4. **Metaphor is concrete.** No abstractions. The derivative problem is a tomb with Roman numerals. The soul is a birthday cake drawn in crayon. The sky is the size of a frog's palm. Every philosophical idea earns its place by becoming a thing you can see, touch, or hold.

5. **The music underneath.** Bach's Ciaccona, the Frog play, Voltaire's rally speech, the walrus documentary — every major scene has a musical or theatrical element that structures its emotional rhythm. When writing connectors, identify the music first.
